## Understanding Course Assignments

### What is a Course Assignment?
A course assignment is when an administrator assigns you to teach or assist with a specific course. Each assignment comes with specific permissions that determine what you can do in that course.

### Types of Teacher Roles

**Primary Teacher**
- Main instructor for the course
- Full access to course management
- Responsible for overall course delivery
- Can manage content, grade, and communicate

**Assistant Teacher**
- Supporting role in the course
- Helps with specific aspects (content, grading, or communication)
- Works under the primary teacher's guidance
- May have limited permissions

**Guest Teacher**
- Temporary or specialized role
- Usually limited to viewing and communication
- Cannot modify content or grades

## Accessing Your Assigned Courses

### Finding Your Courses

**Dashboard View:**
1. Log in to your teacher account
2. Your assigned courses appear on the dashboard
3. Click any course card to access it

**Courses Page:**
1. Navigate to **Courses** in the main menu
2. View all your assigned courses
3. Use filters to find specific courses:
   - Filter by subject
   - Filter by grade level
   - Filter by your role (Primary/Assistant)

### Course Information Display

Each course card shows:
- Course title and description
- Your role (Primary/Assistant)
- Your permissions (badges)
- Number of enrolled students
- Course status (Active/Draft/Archived)
- Last updated date

## Managing Course Content

### If You Have Content Management Permission

#### Creating New Lessons
1. Open the course
2. Navigate to **Content** tab
3. Click **Add Lesson**
4. Fill in lesson details:
   - Lesson title
   - Description
   - Learning objectives
   - Duration
5. Add materials:
   - Upload videos
   - Add documents
   - Embed links
   - Create quizzes
6. Click **Save Lesson**

#### Organizing Content
1. Use drag-and-drop to reorder lessons
2. Group lessons into sections
3. Set prerequisites for lessons
4. Schedule lesson release dates

#### Best Practices
âœ… **Do:**
- Keep content organized and structured
- Use clear, descriptive titles
- Include learning objectives
- Test all links and materials
- Save drafts frequently

âŒ **Don't:**
- Delete content without backup
- Upload copyrighted materials

### If You Don't Have Content Management Permission

You can still:
- View all course materials

- Suggest content improvements to primary teacher
- Report broken links or issues

## Grading and Assessment

### If You Have Grading Permission

#### Grading Assignments
1. Go to **Grading** tab
2. View pending submissions
3. Click on a submission to review
4. Provide feedback and score
5. Submit grade

#### Using the Gradebook
1. Access **Gradebook** from course menu
2. View all student grades
3. Filter by assignment or student
4. Export grades for records
5. Generate progress reports

#### Grading Guidelines
- Be consistent in grading criteria
- Provide constructive feedback
- Grade submissions promptly
- Communicate grading policies clearly
- Keep records of all grades

### If You Don't Have Grading Permission

You can still:
- View student submissions
- See existing grades (read-only)
- Provide informal feedback to primary teacher
- Help identify students needing support

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: How do I know which courses I'm assigned to?**
A: Check your teacher dashboard or navigate to the Courses page. All your assigned courses will be displayed with your role and permissions clearly marked.

**Q: Can I request additional permissions?**
A: Yes, contact the course administrator or your department head to request permission changes. Explain why you need the additional permissions.

**Q: What happens if my permissions change?**
A: You'll receive an email notification immediately. Your dashboard will update to reflect the new permissions. Some features may become available or unavailable.

**Q: Can I decline a course assignment?**
A: Contact the administrator who assigned you. They can remove the assignment if necessary.

### Permission Questions

**Q: Why can't I edit course content?**
A: You don't have Content Management permission for this course. Contact the primary teacher or administrator if you need this access.

**Q: I can see grades but can't change them. Why?**
A: You have view-only access to grades. Only teachers with Grading permission can modify grades.

**Q: Can I message students if I'm an assistant teacher?**
A: Only if you have Communication permission. Check your permission badges on the course page.

**Q: What's the difference between Primary and Assistant teacher?**
A: Primary teachers typically have all permissions and overall responsibility. Assistant teachers may have limited permissions based on their role.

### Technical Questions

**Q: I was assigned to a course but can't see it.**
A: Try these steps:
1. Log out and log back in
2. Clear your browser cache
3. Check your email for assignment notification
4. Contact support if issue persists

**Q: My permission badges aren't showing.**
A: Refresh the page. If the issue continues, clear your browser cache or try a different browser.

**Q: Can I access courses on mobile?**
A: Yes, the platform is mobile-responsive. Use your mobile browser or download the mobile app.

**Q: How do I report a problem with my assignment?**
A: Use the Help button in your dashboard or email support@stharoonschool.com with details about the issue.

### Best Practices Questions

**Q: How should I coordinate with other teachers on the same course?**
A: Communicate regularly with the primary teacher. Use the internal messaging system or schedule coordination meetings.

**Q: What should I do if I disagree with course content?**
A: Discuss concerns with the primary teacher first. If unresolved, escalate to the department head or administrator.

**Q: How often should I check my assigned courses?**
A: Check daily during active teaching periods. Review messages, submissions, and announcements regularly.

**Q: Can I suggest improvements to the course?**
A: Absolutely! Share suggestions with the primary teacher or use the feedback form in the course settings.
